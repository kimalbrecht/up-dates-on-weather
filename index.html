Up-Dates on Weather

A metaLAB (at) Berlin research project in collaboration with ZK/U Berlin – Center for Art and Urbanistics that focuses on raising awareness and mobilizing urban climate resilience through case studies, prototypes, and artistic interventions.

Up-Dates on Weather transforms real-time data from ZK/U's local environmental sensors into sensory and visual experiences by creating personalized weather data tattoos.

The installation reimagines how we perceive everyday weather by inviting visitors to compare their impressions with the live data collected on site.

Data visualizations, 3D scans, and heat maps blur the lines between past and future weather, while a temporary tattoo exposes the subtle differences between human perception and sensor readings.

With this transdisciplinary approach it highlights the collective yet individual nature of weather and the delicate balance between precision and interpretation.

The performative installation is composed of a series of interconnected interfaces: a weather antenna, a questionnaire, a waiting screen and a tattoo operator's dashboard.

Every minute the antenna records local weather data of more than 50 different parameters, including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and solar radiation.

While the antenna delivers an objective dataset, we invite participants to share how they perceive the local weather through a series of questions, exploring how data's precision aligns, or contrasts, with individuals.

Once the answers are submitted, a personalized tribal-inspired visualization in accordance with the information gathered by the antenna pops up on the waiting screen, symbolizing a queue for the tattooing.

The individual's sensation becomes a blueprint to display the weather data, shaping the aesthetics of the tattoo. Simultaneously, the tattoo operator can access the visitor's and antenna's data to identify the tattoo that best matches the confluence with the latter.

Visitors can then take a seat in the tattoo chair to obtain a temporary tattoo of their personalized weather data visualization, embodying the fusion of data and personal perception in a tangible and wearable form.

Up-Dates on Weather aims to create an intimate space to both, make environmental data tangible and revisit its own impressions. During the tattooing process visitors and artists meet for an everyday conversation about the weather that with ease moves into a joint reflection on the installation.

Being tattooed then not only affords them to share a unique moment, but also comes with the notion of memory and an emotional connection to the location and the local weather which is recognized with – among others – the skin.

By emphasizing the myriad variations of tattoo visualizations, the project opens up for an unbiased interrogation of erroneous or ambivalent, yet naturally human input juxtaposed to the supposed accuracy of ubiquitous processing devices.

The installation was on display during the Berlin Art Week 2024 as part of “Commons Cosmodrome – Re-Entering the Spaces of ZK/U”.

Over a period of 4 days around 220 visitors shared their impressions of the local weather data and received a personalized tattoo.
